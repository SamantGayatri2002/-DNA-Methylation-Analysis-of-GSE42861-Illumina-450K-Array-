## üß¨ A Quick Guide to Hypomethylation and Hypermethylation

DNA methylation at CpG sites can either increase (**hypermethylation**) or decrease (**hypomethylation**). Both changes can strongly influence immune responses.

---

### üü¶ Hypomethylation

**Definition**  
A reduction in methylation at a CpG site (lower beta value).

**Biological meaning**

- Loosens chromatin structure  
- Often associated with **increased gene expression**, especially at promoters or enhancers  
- Commonly seen during immune activation  
- Can reflect inflammation, cell proliferation, or stress-response signaling  

**In RA**  
Studies repeatedly observe hypomethylation in:

- Cytokine signaling genes  
- MHC region genes  
- T-cell activation pathways  
- NF-Œ∫B‚Äìrelated promoters  

This indicates that RA immune cells may be epigenetically ‚Äúprimed‚Äù toward inflammatory behavior.

---

### üü• Hypermethylation

**Definition**  
An increase in methylation at a CpG site (higher beta value).

**Biological meaning**

- Condenses chromatin  
- Often associated with **reduced gene expression**  
- Can silence genes involved in immune suppression or tolerance  
- May enhance autoimmunity by suppressing anti-inflammatory programs  

**In RA**  
Hypermethylation can appear in genes related to:

- Immune regulation  
- Pathways that normally restrict inflammation  
- Synovial fibroblast growth control  

Together, the imbalance between hypomethylation (activation) and hypermethylation (silencing) contributes to chronic inflammation in RA.

## üß¨ What Have Previous Methylation Studies Shown in RA?

Across many studies, RA-associated CpGs show consistent patterns.

### üîπ Hypomethylation commonly occurs in:

- Innate and adaptive immune signaling  
- TLR (Toll-like receptor) pathways  
- Interferon signaling  
- T-cell receptor activation regions  
- Cytokine promoters (TNF, IL6, IL1 family)  

### üîπ Hypermethylation commonly occurs in:

- Regulatory T-cell function genes  
- Genes controlling apoptosis  
- Pathways limiting synovial fibroblast expansion  

### üîπ Frequently dysregulated pathways include:

- Cell adhesion and migration  
- Antigen presentation  
- Chronic inflammatory signaling  
- Extracellular matrix remodeling  

These reinforce the idea that RA immune cells undergo widespread epigenetic reprogramming.
